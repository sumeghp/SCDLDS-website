Political Economy of Environmental Regulation in India

Environmental regulation seeks to strike a balance between economic growth and negative impact on the environment. For low and middle income countries, like India, effective regulation of the environment is essential for achieving sustainable development. Are environmental regulations in India effective? What determines the quality of environmental regulation?

To answer these questions we combine administrative data on environmental clearances with high resolution satellite images to examine the quality of environmental regulation across different states of India. All projects that could have negative environmental externalities need to seek environmental clearances before commencing project activity.

We use machine learning models to classify images and detect economic activity in sites that are granted environmental clearance. The projects we study range from mining to industrial to infrastructural activity. We use the image classification of the machine learning models to understand the time gap between environmental clearances and the start of economic activity - an important measure of ease of doing business. We aim to examine the political and bureaucratic determinants of time between environmental approvals and start of economic activity.

The second part of the project uses the trained models to detect illegal mining activity that takes place outside the approved project area or prior to environmental clearance being granted. The presence of illegal mining activity is well established but has not been systematically studied across Indian states. This part of the project seeks to understand the quantum of illegal mining activity and its determinants.
