The Darjeeling Revival Project

The Darjeeling Revival Project, funded by Alt Carbon, is an interdisciplinary research initiative aimed at understanding and enhancing natural carbon sequestration through Enhanced Rock Weathering (ERW) processes in the Eastern Himalayan region.

Focused on basalt and other silicate-rich rock dust, the project investigates how these minerals can be used to draw atmospheric CO₂ while simultaneously revitalizing degraded soils and improving crop productivity, particularly in the tea-growing landscapes of Darjeeling. You can learn more about ERW at Alt Labs.

In our collaboration with Alt Carbon, we investigate the distribution of calcium, magnesium, and titanium concentrations in pre- and post-treatment soil samples, using spatial statistics and geostatistical tools such as Gaussian Random Fields and kriging to model the weathering activity across mountain terrain. This allows us to (i) generate high-resolution maps, (ii) guide sampling strategies, (iii) accurately describe the soil composition and spatio-temporal changes in it, and (iv) and predict the most effective treatment zones.

Beyond geostatistical modelling, we are interested in studying the effect of rainfall and weather on soil, building automated tools to support long-term monitoring, and implementing software that helps compute the quantity of carbon captured by analysing geochemical data. Through this line of work, we aim to create a replicable model for data-driven and community-rooted practices for climate action.
